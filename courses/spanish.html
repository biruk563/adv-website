Course Overview

Everything you need to know about our comprehensive Spanish program for beginners

Begin Your Spanish Language Journey

This 12-week course is designed for complete beginners with no prior knowledge of Spanish. Through engaging lessons, interactive activities, and cultural immersion, you'll build a solid foundation in Spanish that will prepare you for further study or basic communication in Spanish-speaking countries.

Practical Conversation Skills

Learn to introduce yourself, order food, ask for directions, and handle everyday situations in Spanish. Our conversation-focused approach ensures you'll be speaking from day one.

Essential Grammar & Vocabulary

Master the fundamental building blocks of Spanish, including present tense verbs, basic sentence structure, and vocabulary for everyday situations. Learn through context, not rote memorization.

Cultural Understanding

Explore the rich cultures of Spanish-speaking countries through authentic materials, music, videos, and cultural discussions. Understanding the cultural context will enhance your language learning experience.

Listening & Pronunciation

Develop your ear for Spanish through listening exercises and perfect your pronunciation with guided practice. Learn to distinguish regional accents and speak with confidence.

Course Curriculum

A comprehensive learning path designed to develop your Spanish language skills from scratch

Module 1: First Steps in Spanish

Begin your Spanish journey with essential greetings and introductions.

  • Spanish alphabet and pronunciation basics
  • Greetings and introductions
  • Numbers 1-100
  • Basic questions and answers
  • Subject pronouns and the verb "ser" (to be)
  • Project: Introduce yourself in Spanish
Module 2: Describing Yourself and Others

Learn to describe people, places, and things in Spanish.

  • Adjectives and gender agreement
  • Colors, physical descriptions, and personality traits
  • The verb "estar" (to be) and the difference from "ser"
  • Possessive adjectives
  • Family vocabulary
  • Project: Describe your family and friends
Module 3: Daily Life and Routines

Express daily activities and routines in Spanish.

  • Regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs in present tense
  • Time expressions and telling time
  • Days of the week and months
  • Daily routine vocabulary
  • Reflexive verbs for daily activities
  • Project: Describe your daily routine
Module 4: Food and Dining

Learn to navigate restaurants and talk about food in Spanish.

  • Food and beverage vocabulary
  • Ordering in restaurants
  • Expressing likes and dislikes with "gustar"
  • Common irregular verbs
  • Cultural aspects of dining in Spanish-speaking countries
  • Project: Role-play restaurant scenario
Module 5: Shopping and Clothing

Master shopping vocabulary and expressions in Spanish.

  • Clothing and shopping vocabulary
  • Colors and sizes
  • Demonstrative adjectives (this, that, these, those)
  • Numbers 100-1000 and prices
  • Making comparisons
  • Project: Shopping dialogue simulation
Module 6: Travel and Transportation

Learn to navigate travel situations in Spanish-speaking countries.

  • Transportation vocabulary
  • Asking for and giving directions
  • Travel accommodations and booking
  • The verb "ir" (to go) and future plans with "ir a + infinitive"
  • Cultural aspects of travel in Spanish-speaking countries
  • Project: Plan a trip to a Spanish-speaking country
Module 7: Health and Wellness

Express health concerns and wellness topics in Spanish.

  • Body parts vocabulary
  • Describing symptoms and illnesses
  • Making appointments
  • Giving advice with "deber" and "tener que"
  • Pharmacy and medication vocabulary
  • Project: Doctor's visit role-play
Module 8: Leisure and Entertainment

Discuss hobbies, interests, and entertainment in Spanish.

  • Hobbies and free time activities
  • Sports and entertainment vocabulary
  • Making plans and invitations
  • Accepting and declining invitations
  • Cultural activities in Spanish-speaking countries
  • Project: Plan a weekend activity with friends
Module 9: Home and Environment

Learn vocabulary related to home, neighborhood, and environment.

  • House and furniture vocabulary
  • Prepositions of place
  • Neighborhood and city vocabulary
  • Environmental terms and expressions
  • The verb "estar" with locations
  • Project: Describe your home and neighborhood
Module 10: Past Experiences

Begin talking about past events in Spanish.

  • Introduction to the preterite tense
  • Regular and common irregular verbs in the past
  • Time expressions for the past
  • Talking about experiences and events
  • Sequencing past events
  • Project: Tell a story about a past experience
Module 11: Future Plans

Express future plans and intentions in Spanish.

  • Future expressions with "ir a + infinitive"
  • Introduction to the simple future tense
  • Making predictions
  • Expressing hopes and wishes
  • Planning vocabulary
  • Project: Describe your future plans
Module 12: Capstone Project

Apply everything you've learned in a comprehensive final project.

  • Review of key grammar concepts
  • Vocabulary consolidation
  • Extended conversation practice
  • Cultural presentation preparation
  • Self-assessment and progress evaluation
  • Project: Multimedia presentation about yourself and your interests in Spanish

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our Spanish for Beginners course

Do I really need zero prior knowledge of Spanish?

Yes, this course is designed for absolute beginners. We start from the very basics, including pronunciation and the alphabet. If you already know some Spanish, you might find the first few modules too basic, but you'll still benefit from the structured approach and conversation practice. If you're unsure about your level, we offer a free assessment to help determine if this is the right course for you.

How much Spanish will I be able to speak after this course?

By the end of this 12-week course, you'll reach approximately an A1-A2 level on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). This means you'll be able to handle basic everyday situations like introducing yourself, ordering food, shopping, asking for directions, and having simple conversations about familiar topics. You'll have a vocabulary of approximately 800-1000 words and understand the fundamentals of Spanish grammar.

How much time should I commit to the course each week?

We recommend dedicating at least 5-7 hours per week to the course for optimal results. This includes 2-3 hours for lessons and guided practice, plus 3-4 hours for self-study, homework, and additional practice. Consistency is key in language learning, so it's better to practice a little each day rather than cramming all your study time into one day per week.

What dialect of Spanish is taught in this course?

Our course teaches standard Spanish (sometimes called Castilian Spanish) as a foundation, but we also expose students to different dialects and regional variations from across the Spanish-speaking world. Our instructors come from different Spanish-speaking countries, so you'll hear various accents throughout the course. For beginners, the differences between dialects are less important than building a solid foundation in the language.

Will I receive feedback on my pronunciation?

Yes, pronunciation feedback is an important part of our course. In the Interactive and Premium plans, you'll receive feedback during live sessions. All students can also use our speech recognition tools for self-assessment. We focus on helping you develop clear, understandable pronunciation rather than aiming for a perfect native accent. Remember that having an accent is completely normal and doesn't prevent effective communication.

What materials do I need for this course?

All essential course materials are provided digitally through our learning platform. You'll need a computer or tablet with internet access, a microphone for speaking practice, and headphones or speakers. We recommend having a notebook for taking notes and practicing writing. Optional supplementary materials like textbooks or workbooks will be suggested but are not required. For the Interactive and Premium plans, you'll also need a webcam for live sessions.
