Course Overview

Everything you need to know about our comprehensive Mandarin Chinese program for beginners

Begin Your Mandarin Chinese Journey

This 12-week course will take you from zero knowledge to basic conversational fluency in Mandarin Chinese. Designed for complete beginners, our curriculum focuses on practical communication skills while introducing you to Chinese characters, pronunciation, and cultural context essential for authentic language use.

Pinyin & Pronunciation

Master the Mandarin sound system and tones through Pinyin, the official romanization system, providing a solid foundation for accurate pronunciation.

Chinese Characters

Learn to recognize and write essential Chinese characters (Hanzi), starting with simple radicals and building to common words and phrases used in everyday conversation.

Conversational Skills

Develop practical speaking abilities through role-plays, dialogues, and interactive exercises that prepare you for real-life situations like ordering food, asking directions, and making friends.

Cultural Context

Gain insights into Chinese culture, customs, and etiquette that are essential for appropriate language use and deeper understanding of the Chinese-speaking world.

Course Curriculum

A structured learning path designed to build your Mandarin Chinese skills from the ground up

Module 1: Pronunciation & Pinyin

Master the sounds and tones of Mandarin Chinese through the Pinyin system.

  • Introduction to Mandarin Chinese and its importance
  • The four tones and neutral tone
  • Initials and finals in Pinyin
  • Pronunciation drills and tone pairs
  • Basic greetings and introductions
  • Project: Record yourself introducing yourself in Mandarin
Module 2: Basic Conversations

Learn essential vocabulary and sentence patterns for everyday interactions.

  • Numbers and counting in Mandarin
  • Personal pronouns and possessives
  • Question words and forming questions
  • Talking about nationality and languages
  • Basic sentence structure
  • Project: Role-play a first meeting conversation
Module 3: Introduction to Chinese Characters

Begin learning to read and write basic Chinese characters.

  • History and evolution of Chinese writing
  • Character components and radicals
  • Stroke order and writing techniques
  • Common characters for everyday use
  • Reading simple signs and labels
  • Project: Write a self-introduction using characters
Module 4: Family & Relationships

Learn vocabulary and expressions related to family and relationships.

  • Family member terms and kinship vocabulary
  • Describing people and relationships
  • Possessive particles and structures
  • Age and numbers in context
  • Cultural aspects of Chinese family structure
  • Project: Create and describe a family tree in Mandarin
Module 5: Food & Dining

Master vocabulary and phrases for ordering food and dining out.

  • Food vocabulary and Chinese cuisine
  • Ordering in restaurants and expressing preferences
  • Measure words for food items
  • Asking for the bill and handling payment
  • Chinese dining etiquette
  • Project: Role-play a restaurant scenario
Module 6: Time & Daily Activities

Learn to discuss time, schedules, and daily routines.

  • Telling time in Mandarin
  • Days of the week and months
  • Talking about daily activities
  • Basic verb conjugation and aspect
  • Making appointments and plans
  • Project: Describe your daily routine in Mandarin
Module 7: Shopping & Numbers

Develop skills for shopping and handling transactions.

  • Shopping vocabulary and phrases
  • Currency and prices
  • Bargaining and negotiating
  • Describing items (size, color, quantity)
  • Measure words for objects
  • Project: Simulate a shopping experience
Module 8: Travel & Directions

Learn essential vocabulary and phrases for travel and navigation.

  • Transportation vocabulary
  • Asking for and giving directions
  • Location words and prepositions
  • Booking accommodations
  • Handling travel emergencies
  • Project: Plan a trip itinerary in Mandarin
Module 9: Weather & Seasons

Discuss weather conditions and seasonal activities.

  • Weather vocabulary and expressions
  • Seasons and seasonal activities
  • Temperature and climate descriptions
  • Making comparisons
  • Chinese festivals and seasonal customs
  • Project: Create a weather report in Mandarin
Module 10: Health & Emergencies

Learn vocabulary and phrases for health situations and emergencies.

  • Body parts and health vocabulary
  • Describing symptoms and illnesses
  • Visiting a doctor or pharmacy
  • Emergency phrases and situations
  • Chinese medicine concepts
  • Project: Role-play a doctor visit scenario
Module 11: Hobbies & Leisure

Discuss interests, hobbies, and leisure activities.

  • Hobby and interest vocabulary
  • Expressing likes and dislikes
  • Making suggestions and invitations
  • Sports and entertainment terms
  • Popular Chinese pastimes
  • Project: Interview a classmate about their hobbies in Mandarin
Module 12: Review & Final Project

Consolidate your learning and demonstrate your skills.

  • Comprehensive review of key vocabulary and grammar
  • Pronunciation refinement
  • Reading and writing practice
  • Conversation practice in various scenarios
  • Resources for continued learning
  • Final Project: Create a 3-5 minute presentation in Mandarin on a topic of your choice

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our Mandarin Chinese Fundamentals course

Is this course suitable for complete beginners?

Yes, this course is specifically designed for complete beginners with no prior knowledge of Mandarin Chinese. We start from the very basics of pronunciation and gradually build your skills. The pace is carefully structured to ensure you develop a solid foundation without feeling overwhelmed.

Do I need to learn Chinese characters?

While our course introduces basic Chinese characters, we primarily focus on spoken Mandarin and Pinyin (the romanization system) in the beginning stages. You'll learn to recognize and write some essential characters, but becoming fully literate in Chinese characters requires longer-term study. Our approach ensures you can start communicating quickly while gradually building character recognition skills.

How difficult is it to learn Mandarin Chinese?

Mandarin Chinese is considered challenging for English speakers due to its tonal nature and writing system. However, the grammar is relatively straightforward with no conjugations, genders, or complex tense systems. Our course breaks down these challenges into manageable components, focusing on practical communication first. With consistent practice and our structured approach, most students are surprised by how quickly they can begin having simple conversations.

What materials are included with the course?

The course includes comprehensive digital learning materials: a course textbook, audio recordings for pronunciation practice, flashcards for vocabulary, writing worksheets for character practice, and access to our online learning platform with additional resources and exercises. Premium plan students also receive personalized feedback on pronunciation and writing assignments.

How much time should I dedicate to this course each week?

We recommend dedicating at least 5-7 hours per week to get the most out of this course. This includes 2 hours of live class time, 1-2 hours for assignments, and 2-3 hours for self-study and practice. Consistent daily practice, even if just for 20-30 minutes, is more effective than cramming several hours once a week.

What level will I reach after completing this course?

After completing this 12-week course, most students reach an A1-A2 level (according to the Common European Framework). You'll be able to handle basic everyday conversations, introduce yourself, order food, ask for directions, discuss simple topics, recognize common signs, and read simple texts with the help of Pinyin. This provides a solid foundation for further study toward intermediate proficiency.
