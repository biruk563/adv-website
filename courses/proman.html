Course Overview

Everything you need to know about our comprehensive Project Management program

Become a Project Management Professional

This 12-week course will equip you with the knowledge, skills, and practical experience needed to successfully manage projects of any size and complexity. Whether you're looking to start a career in project management, advance in your current role, or prepare for PMP® certification, our curriculum provides a solid foundation across all key project management knowledge areas.

Project Management Frameworks

Learn industry-standard methodologies including Waterfall, Agile, Scrum, and hybrid approaches to select the right framework for different project types and organizational contexts.

Project Planning & Execution

Master the art of creating comprehensive project plans, managing resources, mitigating risks, and delivering projects on time, within scope, and under budget.

Leadership & Team Management

Develop essential leadership skills to effectively manage project teams, resolve conflicts, facilitate communication, and drive team performance in diverse project environments.

PMP® Certification Preparation

Prepare for the globally recognized Project Management Professional (PMP)® certification with targeted exam prep, practice questions, and alignment with the PMBOK® Guide knowledge areas.

Course Curriculum

A comprehensive learning path designed to build your project management expertise from the ground up

Module 1: Introduction to Project Management

Understand the fundamentals of project management and its importance in organizations.

  • What is a project? Defining characteristics and constraints
  • The role of the project manager and key stakeholders
  • Project management methodologies overview (Waterfall, Agile, Hybrid)
  • Project lifecycle and process groups
  • Organizational structures and their impact on projects
  • Project: Analyze a case study and identify project characteristics
Module 2: Project Initiation & Charter

Learn how to effectively start a project and establish its foundation.

  • Business case development and project selection
  • Stakeholder identification and analysis
  • Creating a project charter
  • Defining project scope and objectives
  • Establishing project governance
  • Project: Develop a project charter for a real-world scenario
Module 3: Project Planning - Scope & Schedule

Master the techniques for planning project scope and schedule.

  • Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) creation
  • Activity definition and sequencing
  • Schedule development using critical path method
  • Duration estimation techniques
  • Schedule compression methods
  • Project: Create a WBS and project schedule using MS Project or similar tool
Module 4: Project Planning - Budget & Resources

Learn to plan and manage project costs and resources effectively.

  • Cost estimation techniques
  • Budget development and approval
  • Resource planning and allocation
  • Resource leveling and smoothing
  • Procurement planning
  • Project: Develop a project budget and resource plan
Module 5: Risk Management

Develop strategies to identify, analyze, and respond to project risks.

  • Risk management planning
  • Risk identification techniques
  • Qualitative and quantitative risk analysis
  • Risk response strategies
  • Risk monitoring and control
  • Project: Create a risk register and response plan for a case study
Module 6: Project Quality & Communications

Master quality management and effective project communications.

  • Quality planning and metrics
  • Quality assurance and control techniques
  • Communication planning and stakeholder engagement
  • Effective meeting management
  • Status reporting and dashboards
  • Project: Develop a quality management plan and communications strategy
Module 7: Project Execution & Team Leadership

Learn to lead project teams and execute project activities effectively.

  • Team development and motivation
  • Conflict resolution techniques
  • Decision-making models
  • Delegation and empowerment
  • Managing virtual and cross-cultural teams
  • Project: Role-play leadership scenarios and team challenges
Module 8: Project Monitoring & Control

Develop skills to track project performance and implement corrective actions.

  • Earned Value Management (EVM)
  • Performance measurement and KPIs
  • Change control processes
  • Issue management and escalation
  • Corrective and preventive actions
  • Project: Calculate and analyze EVM metrics for a project scenario
Module 9: Agile Project Management

Master Agile methodologies and their application in project management.

  • Agile principles and mindset
  • Scrum framework and roles
  • Sprint planning and execution
  • Kanban and other Agile approaches
  • Scaling Agile for larger projects
  • Project: Plan and simulate a Sprint for a sample project
Module 10: Project Closure & Lessons Learned

Learn to properly close projects and capture organizational knowledge.

  • Project closure activities and checklist
  • Final deliverable acceptance
  • Administrative and financial closure
  • Conducting lessons learned sessions
  • Knowledge transfer and documentation
  • Project: Develop a project closure report and lessons learned document
Module 11: PMP® Certification Preparation

Prepare for the PMP® certification exam with focused study and practice.

  • PMP® exam structure and content outline
  • PMBOK® Guide knowledge areas review
  • Exam strategies and tips
  • Practice questions and mock exams
  • Application process and requirements
  • Project: Complete a full PMP® practice exam with review
Module 12: Capstone Project

Apply all learned concepts in a comprehensive project management simulation.

  • End-to-end project management simulation
  • Managing a complex project scenario
  • Handling unexpected challenges and changes
  • Stakeholder presentations and reporting
  • Final project evaluation and feedback
  • Final Project: Complete a comprehensive project management plan for a real-world scenario

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our Project Management Professional course

Do I need prior project management experience to take this course?

No, this course is designed to accommodate beginners with no prior project management experience. We start with the fundamentals and gradually progress to more advanced concepts. However, if you do have some project experience, you'll still benefit from the structured approach, tools, and techniques that will help you formalize your knowledge and prepare for PMP® certification if desired.

Will this course prepare me for the PMP® certification exam?

Yes, this course is aligned with the Project Management Institute's PMBOK® Guide and covers all the knowledge areas required for the PMP® exam. Module 11 is specifically dedicated to exam preparation, including practice questions and test-taking strategies. However, please note that to be eligible for the PMP® exam, PMI requires specific education and professional experience requirements that must be met separately.

What software or tools will I need for this course?

You'll need access to Microsoft Office (particularly Excel) and ideally Microsoft Project or a similar project management tool. We provide a 60-day free trial of Microsoft Project for students who don't have access. For Agile modules, we use free tools like Trello and Jira (free version). All other templates and tools are provided as part of the course materials.

How hands-on is this course?

This course is extremely hands-on. Each module includes practical exercises and projects that apply the concepts you've learned. You'll create real project artifacts like charters, WBS, schedules, risk registers, and more. The capstone project in Module 12 brings everything together in a comprehensive simulation. By the end of the course, you'll have a portfolio of project management documents that demonstrate your skills to potential employers.

How much time should I dedicate to this course each week?

We recommend dedicating 8-10 hours per week to get the most out of this course. This includes 2-3 hours of video lessons, 3-4 hours for assignments and projects, and 2-3 hours for reading and practice. The course is designed to be flexible, so you can adjust your schedule as needed, but consistent engagement will lead to the best results.

Will this course help me get a job in project management?

Yes, this course is designed with employability in mind. The curriculum covers the most in-demand project management skills that employers are looking for. The Professional plan includes career placement assistance, resume reviews, and interview preparation. Additionally, the portfolio of project artifacts you'll build throughout the course will demonstrate your capabilities to potential employers. Many of our graduates have successfully transitioned into project management roles or advanced in their current positions.
